![my booklet library](booklets.jpg)

As a student I am required to do a lot of reading. Much of the reading assigned to me is distributed in a PDF, and is often a scan of a book. Printing this out usually results in an awful-looking mess of contrast issues and bad spacing. It also wastes a lot of paper. Very very sad situation!

So may I present to you, the booklet! Not only is it formatted nicely in a tiny book, it also only uses 1/4th of the paper, meaning **you can print a 20 page document with only 5 sheets!**

Here is how I make my booklets...
## 1. Source?

Because the provided pdf is often a book scan, I usually go off and find my own PDF of the source online. Once I have it, I use the browser's "print to PDF" feature to get a PDF of only the pages I want.

Sometimes you can't find it online and have to use your professor's scan. In this case, you can use [this online tool](https://www.sejda.com/split-pdf-down-the-middle) to split it so that each page is on its own sheet.
## 2. Booklet it

To turn my pdf into a booklet, I use [BookletFree](https://bookletfree.com/), which is very fast and does all the splicing locally. Make sure to click the download button to get the full PDF instead of printing the preview.
## 3. Printing and folding

Before you print your booklet PDF, make sure you specify **double sided with "flip on short edge!"**

(If you have a non-duplex printer, you can still booklet, you'll just have to print on one side, flip, reload the paper, and then print on the other side.)

Fold the stack of papers hamburger style, and make sure the order is correct.

To bind it together, unfold and place it on top of some cardboard. Pop one or two staples in along the crease and then bend them over with your finger. Be careful not to poke yourself!
## 4. Booklet success!

Enjoy your booklet! Get a nice pen and scrawl all over it. Write in the margins. Underline and circle everything. Give it a loving coffee stain. Stuff it in your jeans back pocket. Then, once you're finished with it, store it proudly on your bookshelf with all of your other booklets. Soon you will have your own booklet library.

(Alternatively, once you're done with your booklet, you could put it in your [paperbox](/blog/paperbox)!)
