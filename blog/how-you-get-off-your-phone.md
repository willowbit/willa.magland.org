---
title: "This is how you get off your phone."
date: 2025-12-04
---

1. Uninstall them. TikTok, Tumblr, Pinterest, and yes, even Instagram. All of them. Oh, but your friends message you on there? Too bad, uninstall it and check your messages via your web browser, if you really have too. Alternatively tell them to grow a spine and message you elsewhere.

Okay, that was unnecessarily mean. But seriously, uninstall them already! Don't even bother reading the next steps if you don't do this one.

2. Turn off notifications for literally everything. Then go back and turn on what you really need, aka, messages. No, not email.

From now on, notifications are a privilege. Each time you get a notification, ask yourself, "was this really a necessary notification?" If not, disable it, mute the group chat, etc., right at that moment.

(There's actually quite the power trip in disabling an app's notifications, it feels really good.)

3. Take your phone off silent/vibrate and set notification volume to freakishly loud. Now that you're not getting a bajillion notifications every second, make sure you KNOW when you do get one. This way you're not habitually checking your phone every minute to check for new notifs.

YMMV with this one. If it's better to have it "out of sight, out of mind" on silent, do that.

4. Fill in the 6+ hours of your day you've now reclaimed. Do anything and everything in your power to avoid picking up your phone. Start carrying around a journal, a book, a camera, trinkets to gander at, whatever you can find. Fill your walls with art, make your world interesting. Use your eyes, lift up your head, write like a crazy person, be a freak. Anything but glued to your phone.

Sincerely, a fellow phone addict.
