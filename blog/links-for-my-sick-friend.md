COVID?? In 2024? Yes, it is very much [still around](https://www.health.pa.gov/topics/Documents/Diseases%20and%20Conditions/COVID%20MONTHLY%20TRACKING%20REPORT%20December%202023.pdf), and making quite a comeback. And so, it is with great sadness, that my friend has come under this such ailment.

So, dearest friend, I've looked through my links for a few things to entertain you during these dreary days.

1. **In the case that you get bored of Netflix** (I haven't looked through their new stuff recently, is it any good?), [Wikiflix](https://wikiflix.toolforge.org/#/) is a free little site for watching movies that have fallen into the public domain. Maybe better for browsing, but there are certainly some interesting things in there. Also see [WikiVibes](https://wikivibes.toolforge.org/#/), same thing, but for music (and speaking of music, there's also [this](https://headphonecommute.com/best-of-lists/headphone-commutes-best-of-2023/)).

2. By all means *avoid this* if you're feeling susceptible to a headache, but here are a bunch of [mesmerizing computer animations](https://bleuje.com/randomanimations/) by [Etienne Jacob](https://bleuje.com/).

3. [**101 hidden gems: the greatest films you’ve never seen**](https://www.bfi.org.uk/sight-and-sound/lists/101-hidden-gems-greatest-films-youve-never-seen). Believe me, there wasn't a single film on here that I'd even remotely heard of. I think it would be a fun use of a wheel-spinner, and go in completely blind.

4. Bored on your phone? Here's a [curated list of "No Bullshit" games](https://nobsgames.stavros.io/android/). A favorite of mine is [Seedship](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.johnayliff.seedship), a text adventure where you play as an AI trying to save humanity. Kinda morbid, but I really enjoyed it. You can sort by genre, paid or unpaid, and rating.

5. [The Whimsical Web: Websites that spark joy](https://whimsical.club/). This is a great collection to browse through, probably best on a laptop.

6. Fun stuff I've posted previously but recommend:

    * [Motion Extraction](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NSS6yAMZF78&feature=youtu.be) - can't recommend this enough
    * [Nile Rodgers on Tiny Desk Concert](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pRERgcQe-fQ) (actually, all of tiny desk concert is great for when you're sick)
    * [Baumgartner Restoration](https://www.youtube.com/@BaumgartnerRestoration/videos) - dude who restores old paintings

I hope among this you'll find something that appeases the big 'VID-19. Looking forward to seeing you BACK and HEALTHY Monday next week! (🤞)

Love,

Willa
