I study history which means I do a lot of writing. Although I'm a tech buff, most of my learning is done exclusively on paper. As a result, my desk is a torrent of paper scraps, torn out pages, sticky notes, all holding ideas, dates, drafts, phrases, and strange revelations. Usually when I go through this I'm able to sort out what is trash and what is worth saving. However, sometimes a scrap isn't quite useful, but not quite worth the bin. These scraps go in my **paperbox**.

## a paperbox?

A paperbox is my own invention. It's a place to hold paper-based materials (and other stuff to), acting as an unbound journal of words and ideas. In it I put those scraps I talked about, but also other things like received and unsent letters, ripped-out doodles, poems, printed pages, zines, sale receipts, jotted-down quotes, to-do lists... you get the idea. 

## materials

In order to make a paperbox you will need:
- approximately **15 minutes** of time
- an empty **shoebox**
- various paper-based ephemera as described (letters, post-it notes, ticket stubs, etc.)

## instructions

1. open the shoebox you have chosen
2. place your collected paper materials in the shoebox
3. close the shoebox
4. hide it somewhere (mine goes under my desk)
5. OPTIONAL BONUS: decorate your shoebox with stickers and markers to give it some extra pizzazz!

## OK what now?

Now? Nothing, it just sits there and grows and grows. Over time, you will forget about the things you put in there, the things you absentmindedly tossed inside after a long day. After some time, when you're feeling gloomy, creatively stumped, or just bored, crack that baby open and rifle through it! You'll find forgotten ideas, old friends, scribbled words, and most importantly, **proof of your very existence!** Which, a lot of the time, is all we really need a reminder of.

~ Willa
