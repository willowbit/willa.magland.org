Today I watched Hitchcock's _Strangers on a Train_ based on last month's bookbug book by Patricia Highsmith ([brief review here](/bookbug/strangers-on-a-train-patricia-highsmith)), and what do I find? The final scene takes place on a [CAROUSEL](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=75ACQveD9ac)!

This reminded me of a mental list I've been making over the past few years of media with carousels with them (not just in the background, but as something that interacts with the plot). This is my list so far:

- _Catcher in the Rye_ by J.D. Salinger
- _Something Wicked This Way Comes_ by Ray Bradbury
- [_Everybody Rides the Carousel_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Everybody_Rides_the_Carousel) directed by John Hubley
- _Strangers on a Train_ directed by Alfred Hitchcock

I know a list like this probably already exists, but it's fun to add them as I naturally find them.
