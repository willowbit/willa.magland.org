> Years ago, when House of Leaves was first being passed around, it was nothing more than a badly bundled heap of paper, parts of which would occasionally surface on the internet. No one could have anticipated the small but devoted following this terrifying story would soon command...

That's the narrative you'll find on the inside flap of Mark Z. Danielewski's *House of Leaves*. Although brushed off by most as a piece of fuction, synonymous with the book's strange meta-existence within itself, the anecdote does have some truth to it.

> So I just found a crummy URL, all kinds of backslashes and forward slashes and tildes and posted it as a pdf file and said, "Look, if you want to read it, you can get it online."
> And eventually, some strangers (found the book)...(which revealed) how wide a range of readers there was out there that might not necessarily walk into a bookstore or read the New York Times. — [Flakmag Interview](https://web.archive.org/web/20060206024916/http://flakmag.com/features/mzd.html)

Thus explaining the novel's state as a "Second Edition," despite there having been no official previous publication. The first edition Danielewski describes mirrors the edition we later see the band carrying later in the novel:

> Fortunately, after studying me for a moment, presumably making one of those on-the-spot decisions, the drummer shook his head and explained that the lyrics were inspired by a book he'd found on the Internet quite some time ago.

> "Take a look for yourself, " he said, handing me a big brick of tattered paper. "But be careful," he added in a conspiratorial whisper. "It'll change your life." — *House of Leaves*, p513.

So you're telling me the first printing blue edition on my bookshelf is *not* the first release? You've got to be kidding me.

Naturally, it would be any House of Leaves fan's dream to find this first edition, the edition closest to the one Johnny allegedly carried with him. After all, if Danielewski had at one point uploaded it to the web, it has to be around somewhere, right? Let's get digging!

## The Forums

The first place you might look is Reddit, which hosts a healthy (yet incredibly redundant) House of Leaves community. Alas, most of the posts about the elusive First Edition claim that it's all just a part of the book's fiction, which we know is false. The next place you might check is the forums, hosted by Danielewski himself.

Here we find multiple threads regarding the subject, each with varying levels of discussion. Possibly the most reputable source of information on the subject, an article published in the FIRSTS magazine, is mentioned [here](https://forums.markzdanielewski.com/forum/house-of-leaves/house-of-leaves-aa/6009-distribution-history-of-house-of-leaves), along with the claim that the book was originally available on MZD's personal website at http://www.loop.com/~mzd/, "or something like that."

From the FIRSTS article, which you can access a scan of [here](http://www.biondirarebooks.com/pdfs/falcon.pdf), the existence of a pre-publication First Edition is once again confirmed, having circulated through "the underbellies of Los Angeles, Las Vegas and San Francisco, [including] strip clubs and recording studios." It also references that "now-defunct URL."

(In another interview quoted by a user [here](https://forums.markzdanielewski.com/forum/house-of-leaves/house-of-leaves-aa/4102-1st-online-net-version-of-hol?p=70251#post70251), Danielewski goes into more detail about the proliferation of the first edition. I'm pretty sure the interview in question is [this one](https://web.archive.org/web/20060321110843/http://boeken.vpro.nl/artikelen/9323059/), but unfortunately WayBack machine did not capture the audio file, and VPRO Boeken has not responded to my request.)

## The URL

So, we know that, without a doubt, the first edition did exist on the internet at one point, and was probably quite popular. We also know that this web edition is different from the serialized version made available on iUniverse.com (which is, ironically, just as lost as the first one).

The format of the first edition is also still up for debate. While the FlakMag interivew indicates that it was uploaded in the form of a PDF, THX 1138 suggests [a more abstract, hypertext format](https://forums.markzdanielewski.com/forum/house-of-leaves/house-of-leaves-aa/4102-1st-online-net-version-of-hol#post70196), suggesting two "first editions." If Danielewksi were to put pieces of House of Leaves on the internet prior to his involvement at Pantheon, a hypertext format would make sense, considering he wrote the entire book in pencil:

> I didn’t write House of Leaves on a word processor. In fact, I wrote out the entire thing in pencil!

...and typeset the whole thing himself at Pantheon HQ:

> despite all the technological advantages currently available, the latter stages of getting House of Leaves ready for production involved such a great deal of work that Pantheon began to wonder if they were going to able to publish it the way I wanted. So I wound up having to do the typesetting myself.

So, with the mirage of information here, let's take a look at our URLS.

In his proposition of a hypertextually formatted House of Leaves, THX 1138 mentions www.earthlink.net, an ISP founded in 1997, which is around the time Danielewski uploaded HoL to the internet. However, none of WayBack's archived www.earthlink.net URLs contain the loop/ path, not to mention "mzd."

And now we find ourselves where we began, with "http://www.loop.com/~mzd," which is undoubtedly (at least one location) where the first edition had been uploaded. This is confirmed by the various references made to the URL both in [this webring](https://web.archive.org/web/20030106055328/http://www.bomis.com:80/rings/Mpoe-band_related-arts/) and [the Angry Psychos ML archive](http://www.smoe.org/lists/angry-psychos/).[^1]

The loop address WAS crawled by WayBack in [early 1999](https://web.archive.org/web/20110401000000*/http://www.loop.com/~mzd), but resulted in a 404. This suggest that either that:

1) Danielewski took down the website
2) ...or, the more likely case, that http://www.loop.com/~mzd is a shortcut to a longer URL / internal path, suggested [here](https://forums.markzdanielewski.com/forum/house-of-leaves/house-of-leaves-aa/5620-internet-promotional-version?p=120989#post120989).

I think the latter is more plausible, considering that WayBack throws similar 404s for other [valid](https://web.archive.org/web/20051201000000*/http://www.loop.com/~condor/) [homepages](https://web.archive.org/web/20030715000000*/http://loop.com/~bluesky), and that, per his corrospondance with Larry McCaffery, Danielewski used mzd@loop.com for email up to June 2000.[^2] It's likely that The Loop was configured strangely / had anti-crawler measures set up that resulted in the Alexa crawler to hit a brick wall for all member's pages.

## Conclusion

We know that 1) some pre-mature form of House of Leaves existed on www.loop.com/~mzd, and 2) the content of this page was not archived. As of right now, the only way the First Edition might arise is if someone somehow made a backup on their personal computer, which is entirely possible, considering that some printed it out themselves.

But who knows, perhaps if you were to ask around the strip clubs and tattoo parlors of Los Angeles, you might happen upon somebody who knows somebody who keeps a certain heap of bundled A11 paper with them, scrawled with notes, sticky with spilled beer coverless, and a blue business card bookmarked between the pages...
